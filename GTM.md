# GTM — SpendScan AI

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5–25 person seed/Series A startup. They manage the engineering budget, feel vague guilt about their AI tool subscriptions, and have never done a structured audit. They're paying for 3–5 AI tools simultaneously, some of which overlap. They're not price-sensitive enough to cancel manually, but they'd act on a specific recommendation with a dollar number attached.

**Secondary:** Founder running a technical side project or small agency with 2–5 developers who signed everyone up for Cursor Pro and GitHub Copilot individually without checking for plan overlap.

## What They Google Before Wanting This Tool

- "cursor vs github copilot which is better"
- "is claude pro worth it for a team"
- "reduce ai tool costs startup"
- "chatgpt team vs enterprise pricing"
- "windsurf vs cursor 2025"
- "ai tools for developers cost comparison"

These are comparison and cost-validation queries — they're already evaluating, not just curious.

- **r/cscareerquestions** — junior devs asking about tool costs, which surfaces to managers

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Seeding**
1. Post an "Ask HN: What does your team spend on AI tools per developer?" thread. Don't pitch the tool — just ask. Engage every response. After 48h, comment with "I built something to help figure this out" with the link.
2. DM 20 indie hackers in the IH Slack with: "Hey, I built a 2-minute AI spend audit — mind taking a look? I'm trying to validate if this is actually useful." Target people who recently posted about costs.
3. Post in r/ExperiencedDevs: "I audited my team's AI spend and found we were paying for Cursor AND Copilot simultaneously. Built a tool so others can check — [link]"

**Week 2: Amplification**
4. Share the tool in 5 relevant Discord servers (#tools channels in developer communities). Write a short genuine post, not a promo blast.
5. Reach out to 3 developer-focused newsletter writers (TLDR Tech, DevOps Weekly, Bytes.dev) with a short pitch. These newsletters have 50k–200k readers and actively look for free dev tools to feature.

**Week 3: Content**
6. Write a Twitter thread: "We analyzed 47 startup AI tool setups. Here's what we found: [data from early audits, anonymized]". Data-driven threads get high engagement in this community.
7. Submit to Product Hunt. Build a small launch crew of 15–20 people who will upvote day 1.

**Week 4: Referral**
8. Email the first 50 users who left their email: "You used SpendScan last week. If you found it useful, share it with one person." Simple ask, no reward needed at this stage.
